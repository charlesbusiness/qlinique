Obstetric / Abdominal Examination
Fundal Height Measurement (Symphysis-Fundal Height - SFH)

Timing: Routinely measured at every visit starting around 20 weeks. Technique: using a non-elastic tape measure, measured from the top of the pubic bone (symphysis pubis) to the top of the uterine fundus. Clinical rule: between 20 and 36 weeks, fundal height in cm typically correlates directly to gestational age in weeks (± 2 cm). A discrepancy triggers an ultrasound evaluation for growth issues or amniotic fluid abnormalities.




Leopold's Maneuvers (Abdominal Palpation)

Timing: usually performed from 32 to 36 weeks onward, as the fetus grows larger. Purpose: a sequence of four manual palpations to determine fetal lie, presentation, position, and engagement.













Fetal Heart Rate (FHR) Auscultation

Timing: audible via handheld Doppler starting around 10-12 weeks. Normal range: 110 to 160 beats per minute (bpm). Clinical tip: the provider must simultaneously palpate the maternal radial pulse to confirm they are counting the fetal heart rhythm and not the mother's.




Pelvic & Vaginal Examination

Initial visit: may include a speculum exam for a Pap smear (if due), screening swabs for STIs, and a bimanual exam to estimate early uterine size. Third trimester (near term): digital vaginal exams are only performed closer to the estimated date of delivery (or if preterm labor is suspected) to assess cervical dilation, effacement, and fetal station. Note: avoided if the patient presents with unexplained vaginal bleeding.
